# Usability Test Results

## Overview

I conducted usability testing with 3 users to evaluate how easily they could navigate the healthcare app design and complete key actions.

## Test Questions

1. How would you check your health score?
2. How would you book a doctor?

---

## User 1 (Yasser)

* Observations:

  * Quickly focused on the main dashboard.
  * Noticed the health score immediately without guidance.

* Confusions:

  * Slight hesitation before identifying the "Book Doctor" option.

* Feedback:

  * Suggested making the doctor booking option more visually distinct.

---

## User 2 (Dody)

* Observations:

  * Easily identified the health score from the home screen.
  * Navigated smoothly through the interface.

* Confusions:

  * Initially unsure whether to tap "View Alerts" or "Book Doctor" for medical help.

* Feedback:

  * Suggested clearer separation between alerts and actions (e.g., doctor booking).

---

## User 3 (Seyhan)

* Observations:

  * Understood the layout quickly.
  * Found the health score without any difficulty.

* Confusions:

  * Took a moment to locate the doctor booking feature.

* Feedback:

  * Suggested adding icons or stronger visual cues to guide actions.

---

## Key Findings

* Users found it easy to:

  * Locate and understand the health score.
  * Navigate the home screen layout.

* Users were confused about:

  * The exact location and emphasis of the doctor booking feature.
  * The difference between alerts and actionable steps.

---

## Improvements to Consider

* Increase visual emphasis on the "Book Doctor" button.
* Add clearer action hierarchy between alerts and booking actions.
* Introduce stronger visual cues (icons, colors) for primary actions.
